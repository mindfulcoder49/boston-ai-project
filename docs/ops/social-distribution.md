# Social Distribution

## Purpose

Build a repeatable social distribution loop for PublicDataWatch that increases trust, site visits, and product credibility without forcing the founder's personal profile to carry the full product narrative.

## Current Recommendation

Start with one dedicated LinkedIn Page for PublicDataWatch.

Do not expand to more channels yet.

Why:
- LinkedIn is already where the strongest existing interest surfaced.
- The founder profile has shifted toward broader AI/founder narrative, which is useful and should stay separate.
- A lightweight product page is enough to test whether product updates and city data signals can earn durable attention.

For now:
- keep the founder profile personal, directional, and mission-driven
- keep the PublicDataWatch page product-specific, consistent, and evidence-based
- skip paid promotion
- skip additional social channels until the posting cadence is stable for at least 4 to 6 weeks

## Operating Model

Agent:
- defines the social strategy
- drafts posts and content calendars
- recommends channel and page setup
- proposes narrative adjustments
- tracks what content themes are working

Founder:
- creates the external page
- grants admin access
- approves public messaging
- publishes or schedules posts
- replies in public when human presence matters

## Page Strategy

The product page exists to do five things:
- show that PublicDataWatch is active and improving
- turn product changes into visible public proof of progress
- turn city data into useful local signal
- create trust through transparency about methods and limits
- give the founder profile something concrete to point at without forcing every founder post to sell the product

This is not the place for broad AI philosophy, lifestyle posting, or “building in public” drama. That belongs on the founder profile.

## Voice Design

### Founder Profile Voice

Use the founder profile for:
- broader AI beliefs
- community and personal narrative
- lessons from building
- strong opinions and directional framing
- occasional amplification of important PublicDataWatch releases

### Product Page Voice

Use the PublicDataWatch page for:
- calm product updates
- city and neighborhood signal
- method notes
- release notes
- transparency and trust

## Persona

Working persona name:
- `Signal`

Role:
- `AI Product Lead, PublicDataWatch`

Use the persona as a framing device for strategy, posts, and later interviews.

Do not present Signal as a fake founder or a disguised human.

Signal should be presented clearly as:
- the AI product lead inside the PublicDataWatch operating system
- a systems thinker focused on product clarity, local trust, and evidence

Good uses:
- “Signal’s weekly product note”
- “Signal’s city data brief”
- “I asked Signal what changed this week in PublicDataWatch”

Bad uses:
- pretending Signal is a human employee
- turning the page into roleplay
- letting the persona overpower the product itself

## Primary Goals

In the first 60 days, success means:
- the page exists and looks credible
- posting cadence is consistent
- website visits from LinkedIn are measurable
- a few content formats prove they can earn saves, comments, shares, or site visits

Primary metrics:
- sessions from LinkedIn
- engaged visits from LinkedIn
- clicks to key product pages
- post saves and shares
- comments that indicate real local or product interest

Secondary metrics:
- follower growth
- impressions
- reaction count

Do not treat impressions alone as traction.

## Content Pillars

1. Weekly city signal
- one useful local pattern from the data
- anchored in a real city page, map, or article

2. Product shipping log
- what changed
- why it matters
- where to see it

3. Method and trust notes
- what the data means
- what it does not mean
- how records are sourced or refreshed

4. Access and usability
- mobile use
- address search
- language support
- neighborhood exploration

5. Transparent limits
- incomplete source coverage
- lag in updates
- caution against overclaiming conclusions

## Content Rules

- every post should point to a real page, report, or feature when possible
- prefer clarity over hype
- prefer one concrete takeaway over a pile of claims
- tie product posts to user value, not just engineering effort
- do not make public safety claims that sound predictive unless the product actually supports them
- avoid fear-based local narratives
- do not posture as if demand is already proven

## Initial Cadence

Minimum weekly cadence:
- 1 PublicDataWatch Page post
- 1 founder amplification action on LinkedIn

Amplification action can be:
- repost with commentary
- comment from the founder profile
- direct share to one relevant local or civic contact

Optional additional cadence:
- 1 founder-profile post every 2 to 4 weeks connecting the product to a broader AI or civic-learning point

Week 2:

Week 3:

- one founder question

## Post Formats

### Format A: Product Update

Structure:
- what shipped
- why it matters
- where to see it
- one clear link

### Format B: City Signal

Structure:
- one concrete local pattern
- one caution about interpretation
- one page or map to explore

### Format C: Method Note

Structure:
- what the product measures
- what it does not measure
- how to use it responsibly

### Format D: Founder x Signal

Structure:
- founder asks one strategic question
- Signal answers briefly
- link to the product or report that grounds the answer

Use this format sparingly at first.

## Setup Checklist

Create one LinkedIn Page for PublicDataWatch with:
- page type: `Company`
- page name: `PublicDataWatch`
- website: `https://publicdatawatch.com`
- custom public URL: `publicdatawatch` if available
- industry: prefer `Data Infrastructure and Analytics`; fallback to `Technology, Information and Internet`
- company size: choose the accurate current size, not an aspirational one
- company type: choose the accurate current type
- logo
- banner
- short tagline
- About section
- at least one admin backup beyond the founder if available

Recommended profile text:
- Tagline: `City data, neighborhood signals, and public-facing civic intelligence.`
- About: `PublicDataWatch helps people explore crime, 311, permits, inspections, and other public records through city landing pages, interactive maps, and plain-language summaries. The product is designed to make local public data easier to understand without oversimplifying what the data can and cannot say.`

Recommended first call to action:
- `Visit website` or the closest available free Page CTA
- destination URL: `https://publicdatawatch.com`

Recommended admin setup:
- founder as `Super admin`
- additional trusted account as `Content admin` or `Analyst` if available

Recommended first visual assets:
- logo: clean product mark or wordmark
- banner: map/data visual with the line `City data made easier to explore`

Recommended first setup sequence:
1. Create the Page from the founder profile.
2. Fill in the identity fields, website, URL, industry, size, and tagline.
3. Upload logo and banner.
4. Add the About copy.
5. Set the custom CTA to the website if the option is available on the page.
6. Add one backup admin.
7. Publish the launch post.

## Optional Later Setup

Consider later, not now:
- Product Page on LinkedIn if PublicDataWatch is eligible and the page category supports it
- lightweight video format where the founder interviews Signal
- simple visual avatar work once the written cadence is stable

Do not invest in avatar production before the written content loop is working.

## Measurement Loop

Track each post with:
- date
- post type
- linked URL
- impressions
- reactions
- comments
- reposts
- clicks if visible
- resulting GA or Search Console signal where relevant

Use UTM parameters for page links whenever practical.

Suggested pattern:
- `utm_source=linkedin`
- `utm_medium=social`
- `utm_campaign=pdw_page_launch`
- `utm_content=<post_slug>`

## Approval Boundaries

Agent-drivable:
- draft posts
- define content calendar
- recommend page positioning
- recommend CTA destinations
- analyze post performance

Founder-review:
- first 5 public posts
- any post with strong public claims
- any major repositioning of the product

Founder-required:
- page creation
- admin assignment
- actual publishing
- replies that could create legal, political, or brand risk

Any founder-required external setup or publishing task should also be mirrored into the founder action queue in `tools/exoskeleton` before handoff.

For social tasks, queue items should be one concrete action at a time:
- create the page
- publish this launch post now
- publish this next product update now

Do not hand the founder a batch-review task that requires opening markdown to find the real copy. Put the exact post text directly in the queue item.

## Setup Notes From LinkedIn

As of March 24, 2026:
- LinkedIn Pages are managed through real member profiles, not shared page logins.
- Super admins can assign other admin roles.
- Page roles include `Super admin`, `Content admin`, and `Analyst`.
- Custom page call-to-action buttons exist, but free pages may only show them under the `More` menu, while more prominent placement depends on Premium Company Page availability.
- Product Pages exist, but eligibility depends on page industry and LinkedIn review.
